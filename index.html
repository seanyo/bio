Biography

Sean builds websites and communities, often at the same time. He built his first website in 1996 and he still wakes up thinking about building them. Sean loves connecting with people and helping people connect with each other. For Sean, nothing is better than when people can come together, share, and help each other be more awesome.

Sean spent more than a decade in Higher Education Enterprise IT working on core campus infrastructure from servers to email to web hosting. He co-founded Resonant Studios, a web development company in 2005 which has worked with local companies, non-profits, tech start-ups, Canadian Universities and national brands. Most recently, Sean was Chief Architect at InGamer - a technology startup building a second-screen companion fantasy game for live broadcast sports.

Sean has been on two short-term international volunteer placements, in 2007 in Kathmandu where he delivered web developer training for the Nepal Fair Trade Group and in 2009 in Malang, Indonesia, where he worked with the Agape Social Foundation, building their IT and web curriculum. During these trips, he learned about the real implications of the Digital Divide, the power of hope and the need for capacity development as the foundation for international development work. You can read about his experience at sean.onleave.ca.

Sean is a leader in the tech community of Waterloo and Guelph. He founded and runs the Guelph Web Maker Meetup, and Accessibility Camp Guelph as well as helping run HackGuelph, a meetup for coders, the Guelph PHP Users Group, the Kitchener-Waterloo Web Design Meetup, and the University of Guelph Accessibility Conference. Sean's most recent ventures are Hackademy, a non-profit focused on digital literacy for kids and Hive Waterloo, a network for technology communities modelled on Hive NYC and Hive Chicago.

A frequent technical presenter, Sean has spoken at local, provincial and national technology conferences both in Canada and the US on web development, web accessibility, Wordpress, Drupal and User Experience.

If you'd like to get in touch with Sean, he's always interested in connecting with new people. You can find his contact info and social network info at seanyo.ca.
